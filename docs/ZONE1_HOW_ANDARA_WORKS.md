## 1. What Andara Ionic Actually Is

Andara Ionic is a highly concentrated ionic sulfate mineral solution made from volcanic mineral sources.

**Primary Purpose:** Clarify, clean and energetically organise water using very small amounts of charged minerals.

### Key Points

| Point | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Not a supplement** | Not positioned as mineral supplement or medicine — intended for water treatment |
| **Ionic and sulfated** | Minerals present as charged ions (Fe³⁺, Al³⁺, Mg²⁺, Ca²⁺, SO₄²⁻) in sulfated form |
| **Micro-dosed** | ~1 ml per 1 liter = low final concentration (mg/L range) |

## 2. The 4 Stages in Your Glass of Water

### Stage 1: Initial Contact — Charge "Shock"
- Highly charged ions spread immediately
- Meet particles, colloids, dissolved metals, organic residues
- **Result:** Water's electrical balance changes in seconds

### Stage 2: Coagulation — Tiny Bits Stick Together
- Charged ions neutralise surface charge of particles
- Particles stop repelling, begin coming together
- **Process:** Coagulation (invisible at first)

### Stage 3: Flocculation & Precipitation — Visible Clean-Up
- Small particles gather into visible clusters ("flocs")
- Flocs trap: dirt, turbidity, organic pollution, some heavy metals
- Flocs sink or can be filtered
- **Result:** Clarified, lower-turbidity water

### Stage 4: Conditioning — Sulfate-Organised Water
- Stable sulfate level (~17–30 mg/L reference range)
- More organised charge distribution
- Measurable via: turbidity ↓, ORP shift, conductivity ↑
- **Result:** Ionically balanced water, closer to mineral springs

## 3. Observable Effects

### Visual Changes
- Initial cloudiness → floc formation → settling → clear upper layer
- Fine sediment accumulates at bottom

### Taste & Smell
- Smell: cleaner, more neutral
- Taste: softer, more rounded, mild mineral note

### Measurable Parameters
| Parameter | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Turbidity | Drop in NTU — more optical clarity |
| EC (conductivity) | Often increases — clearer ionic profile |
| ORP | Shifts toward more ordered charge behaviour |
| pH | Remains normal band (concentrate is acidic, final is balanced) |

---

## 4. Why Sulfate Is Central

1. **Bridge ion** — flocculation, natural springs, geological cleaning
2. **Multi-valent cations + sulfate** — Fe³⁺, Al³⁺ + SO₄²⁻ = effective binding
3. **17–30 mg/L reference band** — efficient clarification, stable behaviour

---

## 5. Clarification vs. Conditioning

| Clarification | Conditioning |
|---------------|--------------|
| Remove/bind unwanted particles | Leave water in stable mineral state |
| Coagulation → Flocculation → Precipitation | Sulfate/mineral background |
| Physical/chemical clean-up | Coherent charge behaviour |
